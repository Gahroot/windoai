## The Economics of Speed

### What Speed Means for Revenue

The numbers are clear: first responder wins the job.

| Response Time | Lead Capture Rate | Description |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Under 60 seconds | 85%+ | Gold standard |
| 1-5 minutes | 60-70% | Good but losing ground |
| 5-15 minutes | 30-50% | Marginal |
| 15+ minutes | 10-25% | Poor |

### The Cost of One Missed Call

- Average window replacement job: **$15,000**
- Average full-home window package: **$25,000-$60,000**
- Average entry door installation: **$3,000-$8,000**

**Every missed call isn't just a lost opportunity — it's giving that $15,000+ job to your competitor.**

### The Seasonal Multiplier

Window and door demand peaks in spring and fall:
- Spring (March-June): Homeowners start renovation projects
- Fall (September-November): Pre-winter energy efficiency upgrades
- Post-storm: Emergency window/door replacements surge

Being the first responder during peak season captures disproportionate market share.

## Understanding Window & Door Lead Behavior

### Why Homeowners Call

1. **Energy efficiency:** High utility bills driving upgrade interest
2. **Aesthetics:** Curb appeal and home renovation projects
3. **Damage/wear:** Foggy glass, broken seals, drafts, difficulty operating
4. **Home sale prep:** Upgrading before listing
5. **New construction:** Builder/contractor partnerships

### The Call Pattern

**Research phase (weeks before):**
- Browsing online, reading reviews
- Comparing materials (vinyl, wood, fiberglass, aluminum)
- Getting ballpark pricing

**Action phase (day of call):**
- Ready to schedule an in-home estimate
- Contacting 3-5 companies simultaneously
- Booking with first responder

**Decision phase (1-2 weeks after estimates):**
- Comparing proposals
- Negotiating pricing
- Selecting installer

### What Homeowners Say

> "I called five window companies. The first one who answered scheduled a quote the same day. I never heard from three of the others."

> "We left voicemails at three companies on Saturday. Only one called back — on Tuesday. By then, we'd already signed with someone else."

> "Our windows are 25 years old and we're losing heat. We just need someone who can come look at them this week."

## The 60-Second Standard

### Why Under 60 Seconds?

Research shows:
- After 5 minutes, qualification drops by **80%**
- 78% of buyers choose **first responder**
- Sub-60-second response captures **3-5x more jobs**

### What 60 Seconds Looks Like

**Caller:** "Hi, we're thinking about replacing the windows in our home."

**AI Response (37 seconds later):**
> "Thanks for calling! I'd love to help you get started. Do you know roughly how many windows you're looking to replace, or is this for the whole house?"

**Caller:** "Probably 12-15 windows. The whole first floor plus a few upstairs."

**AI:**
> "Great — that's a solid project. Are your current windows giving you any specific issues, like drafts, condensation between the panes, or difficulty opening them?"

**Caller:** "Yeah, they're foggy and really drafty in winter."

**AI:**
> "That's really common with older windows. I have tomorrow afternoon at 2pm or Thursday morning at 9am available for a free in-home estimate. Which works better for your schedule?"

**Result:** Lead captured, estimate booked, your sales team preserved for actual consultations.

## Project Qualification Protocols

### The Qualification Framework

#### Questions to Ask Every Lead:

1. **Project scope:** How many windows/doors? Whole home or partial?
2. **Current condition:** What issues are they experiencing? (Drafts, fog, damage)
3. **Timeline:** When are they looking to get this done?
4. **Property type:** Single-family, multi-family, commercial?
5. **Decision stage:** Getting quotes, ready to decide, or just researching?

### Project Type Routing

**High Priority (Book same-day/next-day estimate):**
- Full-home window replacement (12+ windows)
- Emergency replacements (broken/damaged)
- Builder/contractor partnerships (volume work)

**Standard Priority (Book within the week):**
- Partial window replacement (3-10 windows)
- Single door installation
- Energy efficiency upgrades

**Nurture (Follow-up sequence):**
- Early research phase
- Price shopping only
- Future project planning

## After-Hours Response Strategy

### The 40% Problem

> **40%+ of window and door inquiries come in outside business hours.**

Evenings and weekends are when homeowners research and make decisions. Saturday morning after seeing a home show ad? Sunday evening after feeling drafts all weekend?

### After-Hours Protocol

**Evening (5 PM - 10 PM):**
- Answer and qualify
- Offer next-day estimate appointments
- Send confirmation with preparation tips

**Overnight (10 PM - 6 AM):**
- Answer and qualify
- Flag for morning team review
- Send immediate confirmation

**Weekends:**
- Full qualification and booking
- Monday appointments if preferred
- Saturday estimates for high-priority leads

## Multi-Channel Follow-Up

### The Follow-Up Sequence

**Immediate (0-5 minutes):**
- Phone call answered by AI
- Qualification conversation
- Estimate booked or follow-up set

**Confirmation (5-15 minutes):**
- SMS: "Your in-home estimate is confirmed for Tuesday at 10am."
- Email: Full details with what to expect, company info

**Reminder (24 hours before):**
- SMS: "Quick reminder — your window estimate is tomorrow at 10am. Still good?"
- Email: Preparation tips (know your window count, any specific concerns)

**Post-estimate (4 hours after):**
- Email: "How did your estimate go? Any questions about the proposal?"

**Post-estimate (48 hours later):**
- If no response: Follow-up with special offer or financing options
- If interested: Fast-track to scheduling
- If comparing: Provide comparison materials

## Vendor Selection Criteria

### Must-Have Features

1. **Sub-60-second response time**
2. **Natural conversation capability** — not scripts or button menus
3. **Window & door industry knowledge** — project types, materials, terminology
4. **Calendar integration** — direct estimate booking
5. **CRM integration** — automatic data sync
6. **24/7 availability** — especially for evening/weekend inquiries

### Red Flags

- **Fixed call capacity** (will get overwhelmed during peak season)
- **Per-minute pricing only** (costs spike with longer qualification calls)
- **Generic platform** (not tuned for window & door conversations)
- **No estimate booking** (just captures info, doesn't convert)
